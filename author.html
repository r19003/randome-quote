A.P.J KALAM

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was a visionary leader known for his contributions to space research and missile technology. His dedication to education and his inspirational speeches continue to inspire millions worldwide.














Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs was a co-founder of Apple Inc., a visionary entrepreneur, and a pioneer in the world of personal computing and technology. His innovative products, like the iPhone and MacBook, revolutionized industries and changed the way we interact with technology. His legacy is marked by his relentless pursuit of excellence and design aesthetics.










Nelson Mandela

Nelson Mandela was an iconic South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and the country's first Black president. His lifelong struggle against racial injustice led to his imprisonment for 27 years. After his release, he became a symbol of peace, reconciliation, and the fight for equality, earning global admiration and the Nobel Peace Prize.










Hellen Keller

Helen Keller was an American author, disability rights advocate, political activist and lecturer. She was a founding member of the American Civil Liberties Union 1. Her autobiography, The Story of My Life (1903), publicized her education and life with Sullivan. It was adapted as a play by William Gibson, and this was also adapted as a film under the same title, The Miracle Worker 1.










Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. was an American Baptist minister, activist, and political philosopher who was one of the most prominent leaders in the civil rights movement from 1955 until his assassination in 1968 1. He advanced civil rights for people of color in the United States through nonviolence and civil disobedience. King participated in and led marches for the right to vote, desegregation, labor rights, and other civil rights 1.
